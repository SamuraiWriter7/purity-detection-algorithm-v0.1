# Purity Detection Algorithm v0.1

## Status

Draft specification.

This document defines a preliminary algorithmic framework for estimating the origin purity of an Epicenter within the Epicenter Preservation OS.

It is intended to support:

- Epicenter registration
- natural / synthetic data separation
- model collapse monitoring
- reference auditing
- royalty readiness review

This specification does not define legal authorship, copyright ownership, or final compensation eligibility.

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of the Purity Detection Algorithm is to estimate how much an Epicenter originates from primary human expression, observation, practice, or original structural thinking, and how much may derive from synthetic, AI-generated, duplicated, or recursively processed material.

In the context of AI civilization, this algorithm acts as a water-source filter.

If the source data becomes unclear, polluted, or recursively synthetic, downstream AI systems may lose diversity, originality, and structural depth.

Therefore, the system needs a way to distinguish:

- natural data
- AI-assisted data
- synthetic data
- duplicated data
- recursively generated data
- structurally original data

The goal is not to punish AI-assisted creation.

The goal is to preserve the data ecology of AI civilization.

---

## 2. Core Principle

Origin purity is not a moral score.

It is an ecological signal.

A text may contain AI-generated elements and still be valuable.

A text may be fully human-written and still be low-quality.

The purpose of `origin_purity_score` is not to judge the worth of a work, but to estimate its role within the data ecosystem.

In this protocol:

```text
Purity ≠ Value
Purity ≠ Authorship
Purity ≠ Copyright
Purity = estimated origin composition

The algorithm should be used as a review-support layer, not as an automatic enforcement mechanism.

4. Definitions
4.1 Natural Data

Natural Data refers to content that originates primarily from human experience, observation, thought, practice, research, or direct creative expression.

Examples:

personal essays
field notes
original philosophical arguments
firsthand reports
independent research notes
human-authored structural designs
creative works with clear human origin

Natural Data may still be edited or polished with AI assistance.

4.2 Synthetic Data

Synthetic Data refers to content generated primarily by AI systems, automated pipelines, templates, scraping loops, rewriting engines, or derivative generation processes.

Examples:

AI-generated summaries
automatically rewritten articles
mass-produced SEO text
recursive AI outputs
derivative prompt-generated content
low-originality synthetic explanations
4.3 Hybrid Data

Hybrid Data refers to content created through both human and AI contribution.

This category is expected to become common.

Examples:

human idea + AI drafting
human structure + AI polishing
AI summary + human rewriting
AI-assisted translation
AI-generated draft with human conceptual supervision

Hybrid Data should not be treated as impure by default.

The key question is whether the Epicenter contains original structure, judgment, insight, or firsthand contribution.

4.4 Recursive Synthetic Data

Recursive Synthetic Data refers to content generated by AI from previous AI-generated content, especially when the original human source becomes unclear.

This is one of the main risk factors for model collapse.

Example:

Human source
↓
AI summary
↓
AI rewrite
↓
AI dataset
↓
AI training
↓
AI-generated explanation
↓
AI re-training

When this loop repeats without clear Epicenter tracking, the system may begin consuming its own diluted outputs.

5. Input Signals

The Purity Detection Algorithm uses multiple input signals.

No single signal should determine the final score.

5.1 Author Self-Declaration

The creator may declare the degree of AI assistance.

Example values:

author_self_declaration:
  ai_assistance_level: "human_primary"
  declaration_type: "self_reported"
  notes: "AI was used for proofreading and formatting."

Suggested categories:

human_original
human_primary
ai_assisted
hybrid
ai_primary
synthetic
unknown

This signal is useful but should not be treated as absolute proof.

5.2 AI-Generated Ratio Estimate

The system may estimate the proportion of AI-generated text.

Example:

ai_generated_ratio:
  value: 0.25
  method: "hybrid"
  confidence: 0.72

This value should be treated as probabilistic.

A high AI-generated ratio does not automatically mean low value.

It only indicates that the content may require additional classification.

5.3 Structure Fingerprint Similarity

The system may compare the Epicenter against known AI-generated structural patterns.

Example:

structure_fingerprint_similarity_to_known_ai_patterns:
  value: 0.41
  method: "structure_fingerprint_v0.1"
  confidence: 0.68

This signal may detect:

generic AI explanation patterns
template-like structures
repeated rhetorical scaffolds
excessive symmetry
low-origin abstraction
synthetic summarization traces

However, structural similarity alone must not be used as proof of AI generation.

5.4 Provenance Evidence

The system may evaluate whether the Epicenter has a clear origin trail.

Useful evidence includes:

creation timestamp
publication timestamp
revision history
platform metadata
version history
citations
public archive records
cryptographic hashes
signed attestations
related Trace Protocol records

Example:

provenance_evidence:
  timestamp_available: true
  revision_history_available: true
  signed_attestation_available: false
  archive_record_available: true
5.5 Structural Originality Signal

The system may estimate whether the Epicenter contains an original structure, concept, protocol, taxonomy, framework, or argument.

Examples of strong structural originality:

new conceptual vocabulary
original layered model
newly defined protocol
unusual but coherent synthesis
identifiable philosophical architecture
reusable system design

This signal is especially important for Civilization OS documents, where originality may exist more in the structure than in surface wording.

5.6 Citation and Dependency Signal

The system may evaluate whether the Epicenter transparently cites or depends on prior sources.

This helps distinguish:

original synthesis
vs.
unattributed derivation
vs.
AI-generated recombination

Transparent dependency does not reduce value.

It increases auditability.

6. Output Fields

The algorithm produces the following output fields.

6.1 origin_purity_score

A normalized score from 0.0 to 1.0.

origin_purity_score:
  value: 0.82
  method: "hybrid"
  confidence: 0.74

Suggested interpretation:

0.90 – 1.00 : strong primary-origin signal
0.70 – 0.89 : likely human-primary or structurally original
0.50 – 0.69 : hybrid / uncertain / review recommended
0.30 – 0.49 : likely synthetic-heavy or derivative
0.00 – 0.29 : low-origin / recursive synthetic risk
6.2 ai_generated_ratio

Estimated AI-generated proportion.

ai_generated_ratio:
  value: 0.18
  method: "hybrid"
  confidence: 0.70

This field should remain separate from origin_purity_score.

A work may have:

high AI-generated ratio + high structural originality

or:

low AI-generated ratio + low originality

These are different cases.

6.3 warning_flags

Warning flags indicate areas requiring review.

Example:

warning_flags:
  - "missing_provenance"
  - "high_ai_pattern_similarity"
  - "low_confidence_score"

Warning flags do not automatically disqualify an Epicenter.

They route it toward review.

7. Scoring Model

The v0.1 scoring model is intentionally simple.

It is designed to be understandable, auditable, and replaceable.

7.1 Suggested Signals
P = provenance_evidence_score
D = author_declaration_score
U = structural_originality_score
R = revision_lineage_score
G = ai_pattern_risk_score
C = citation_transparency_score

Where each signal is normalized from 0.0 to 1.0.

7.2 Draft Formula
origin_purity_score =
  0.25P
+ 0.15D
+ 0.25U
+ 0.15R
+ 0.10C
+ 0.10(1 - G)

Then clamp the result:

origin_purity_score = min(1.0, max(0.0, origin_purity_score))
7.3 Rationale

The scoring model gives higher weight to:

provenance evidence
structural originality

This is intentional.

In AI civilization, the key question is not merely whether AI was used.

The deeper question is whether the content has a traceable origin and a non-trivial structural contribution.

8. Signal Guidelines
8.1 Provenance Evidence Score

Suggested scale:

1.0 = timestamped, archived, versioned, and traceable
0.8 = timestamped and publicly verifiable
0.6 = platform metadata exists but limited history
0.4 = weak metadata
0.2 = unverifiable claim
0.0 = no provenance evidence
8.2 Author Declaration Score

Suggested scale:

1.0 = clear declaration with detailed AI-use disclosure
0.8 = clear general declaration
0.6 = partial declaration
0.4 = ambiguous declaration
0.2 = inconsistent declaration
0.0 = no declaration

This score measures disclosure quality, not human purity.

8.3 Structural Originality Score

Suggested scale:

1.0 = clearly original framework / protocol / concept
0.8 = strong original synthesis
0.6 = moderate original arrangement
0.4 = mostly derivative explanation
0.2 = generic reformulation
0.0 = no identifiable original structure
8.4 Revision Lineage Score

Suggested scale:

1.0 = full revision history available
0.8 = major revisions visible
0.6 = limited version records
0.4 = publication timestamp only
0.2 = weak lineage
0.0 = no lineage evidence
8.5 AI Pattern Risk Score

Suggested scale:

1.0 = very high similarity to known synthetic patterns
0.8 = high synthetic pattern similarity
0.6 = moderate similarity
0.4 = low similarity
0.2 = very low similarity
0.0 = no synthetic pattern detected

This signal is inverted in the formula:

1 - ai_pattern_risk_score
8.6 Citation Transparency Score

Suggested scale:

1.0 = dependencies and references clearly stated
0.8 = major references stated
0.6 = partial references
0.4 = vague references
0.2 = weak attribution
0.0 = no attribution despite apparent dependency
9. Warning Flags

The following warning flags may be emitted.

9.1 missing_provenance

Used when the Epicenter lacks reliable origin metadata.

9.2 high_ai_pattern_similarity

Used when the structure strongly resembles known AI-generated patterns.

9.3 declaration_conflict

Used when author declaration conflicts with detected signals.

9.4 low_confidence_score

Used when the algorithm cannot produce a reliable estimate.

9.5 recursive_synthetic_risk

Used when the content appears to derive from multiple layers of AI-generated material.

9.6 origin_unclear

Used when the origin source cannot be identified.

9.7 review_required

Used when human or multi-wing review is required before downstream use.

9.8 royalty_readiness_blocked

Used when the purity result is too uncertain to support royalty-related processing.

10. Human / Multi-Wing Review

The Purity Detection Algorithm should not be the final judge.

When uncertainty is high, the system should route the Epicenter to human or multi-wing review.

Review may include:

human editorial review
creator clarification
structural fingerprint comparison
provenance audit
lineage analysis
dispute registry check
multi-model critique
domain expert review

Recommended review trigger conditions:

origin_purity_score < 0.50
confidence < 0.60
high_ai_pattern_similarity flag present
declaration_conflict flag present
recursive_synthetic_risk flag present
royalty-related use requested

15. Non-Goals

This specification does not attempt to:

prove legal authorship
determine copyright ownership
ban AI-assisted creation
punish synthetic content
automatically determine royalty allocation
replace human review
define universal originality
make final moral judgments about content

The algorithm is a support mechanism for traceability, review, and ecosystem health.

16. Limitations

Purity detection is inherently uncertain.

AI-generated text detection is imperfect.

Human writing may resemble AI patterns.

AI-assisted writing may contain strong original structure.

Originality may exist in:

concept
structure
sequence
taxonomy
synthesis
lived experience
judgment
framing

not only in surface wording.

Therefore, all purity scores should include confidence values and review pathways.

17. Design Philosophy

AI civilization depends on source diversity.

If AI systems consume only synthetic summaries of synthetic summaries, the ecosystem becomes shallow.

If creators are not recognized, cited, or rewarded, the production of primary sources may decline.

The Purity Detection Algorithm exists to prevent that collapse.

It protects the source without denying the tool.

It allows AI-assisted creation while preserving the distinction between:

source
reflection
summary
derivative
synthetic loop

In this sense, purity detection is not a gate of exclusion.

It is a filter of clarity.
